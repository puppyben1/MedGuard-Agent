# Design

<!-- impeccable:design-schema 1 -->

## Visual World

MedGuard-Agent uses a clinical instrument world: cool white laboratory surfaces, graphite text, quiet cyan instrumentation, and restrained risk color. The memorable object is a translucent Halo scan ring that suggests a pill, a lens, a safety radar, and a knowledge-graph orbit at once.

## Interface Principles

- The first viewport demonstrates risk analysis rather than marketing it.
- Clinical readability beats decorative motion; animation must mean scan, route, or verification.
- Data source status, evidence coverage, and uncertainty must stay visible near actions.
- Cards are reserved for repeated operational units and reports; page regions remain broad, calm bands.
- Chinese clinical copy is primary, with English labels used only for technical modes and product vocabulary.

## Composition

The shell opens with a split clinical scan deck: left-side sequence/navigation, a large animated Halo object in the center, and concise product proof plus system readiness on the right. Below it, a segmented workbench keeps existing workflows intact: prescription review, drug-safety Q&A, ADR analysis, research mining, and configuration.

## Color And Type

The page is light because competition review and clinical demo use happen in bright rooms on projected or shared screens. Backgrounds use off-white and mist gray; text uses slate/graphite; cyan marks active instrumentation; emerald means ready; amber means missing configuration; red/orange are reserved for clinical risk.

Type uses the system UI stack for dependable Chinese rendering. Display text is large but compact, with no negative letter spacing. Measurement-like labels use smaller system text rather than a decorative monospace costume.

## Motion

The signature motion is one Halo scan: a slow rotating elliptical lens with a passing sweep and small evidence nodes. Secondary motion is limited to active nav highlights, status pulses, and loading states that already exist in workflow components.
